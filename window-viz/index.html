Window Visualization
Visualization inspired by the light through the windows on Upper West 110th street, New York.
Data provided by caniuse, Google Chrome Youtube Channel and Unsplash.
Designed By Zhufeng Xu (Alan).
Transformation
Photo taken by myself.
The typical windows on 110th street consist of 2 glass. There're 3 visible states of the light inside: - Lights off - Lights half-on - Lights all on Correspondingly, the windows show 3 different states:
Both pieces of the glass are black
The lower piece of the glass lights up (lights half-on or half shaded by the curtain)
Both pieces of the glass light up
It's not hard to come up with data encodings related to this. For example, we can map:
Things that are not even started
Half-done things
Fully completed things
I can't wait to apply these windows to the real-world scenario.
Scenario
The first scenario I think of is the support of features in certain browsers.
We have different choices when choosing favourite browsers. However, there're underlying competition in tech field.
Standards made by W3C are updating frequently. Features coming from the community are of the most practical value. Also, they bring challenges to browsers to update their functionality in time.
Features in the browser can be in full support, partial support, or no support state.
Consequently, using the data encoding above can be pretty suitable.
Here, we're going to check the support status of 4 features, which are transform, flexbox, gridbox feature from CSS3, and service worker from Browser API(Javascript).
Also, 4 mainstream browsers are selected for testing:
Chrome
Safari
